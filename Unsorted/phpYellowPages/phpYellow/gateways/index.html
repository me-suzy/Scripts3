Choose a Processor

Payment Validation - How it works

Not all processors are created equal. Some are worse than others. Much worse. Payment gateway processors make banks look good. Beware of processors and carefully watch their billing to your account, and to your credit card too.

You know I actually read the entire agreement the last time the payment processor said they changed it. I have formal legal education from a university. Nowhere did I read that the processor was going to hit up my credit card instead of my positive online account balance. But they did hit up my credit card and took hundreds. Before that they completely missed a 4 week period of payment involving well over a thousand dollars US funds. Not even an apology. I'd never even have known they had missed two (2) payments (each covering a two week period and in theory automatically transferred into my account - yeah right...) if I didn't watch the account numbers like a hawk. And Customer Service was non existent - there wasn't even an 800 number that was usable and I racked up over USD$80 in one month of phone calls to the VP of Customer Service - just to get the money that I was paying them to collect for me. hmmmm. Did you check the fine print about chargebacks to your account? If you didn't it could cost you hundreds each month ... it's not the processor who you pay and who approves the transaction who is reponsible for it. You are! And if the transaction later goes sour guess who pays for the transaction that the processor approved ... YOU. That's not all, but perhaps that's enough to save you from the incompetent, insensitive, non-caring and fraudulent rip off artists that call themselves payment gateway processors. Buyer beware.
